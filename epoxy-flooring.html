Epoxy Flooring

About Our Company

Not all "epoxy" is actually epoxy. It’s helpful to distinguish between the various chemical makeups:

  • 100% Solids Epoxy: The professional standard for thickness and durability. It’s excellent for filling cracks and pits in older concrete.
  • Polyurea: Often considered better than epoxy for many DIYers because it’s UV stable (won't yellow), more flexible, and has an easier installation window.
  • Moisture Cured Urethanes (MCU): Products like TruAlloy offer high abrasion resistance and are a great alternative to standard floor paints.

Why "Garage Floor Paint" is a Trap

A common mistake for homeowners is buying cheap kits from big-box stores. You might want to emphasize that garage floor paint is thin and prone to "hot tire pickup," where the heat from your tires literally peels the paint off the floor. Real epoxy or polyurea bonds chemically to the concrete.

The Golden Rule: Surface Preparation

As the experts say, "Hot tire pickup is Latin for poor floor prep." For any coating to last, the concrete must be:

Cleaned

Removing all oil and chemicals.

Profiled

Either through acid etching or mechanical grinding to ensure the coating has a "tooth" to grab onto.

Repaired

Using concrete fillers for cracks before the main coat goes down.
